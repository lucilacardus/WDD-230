Three days before the summer solstice, it’s snowing in parts of the West

Sometimes, Mother Nature has a sense of humor. It certainly seemed like that in parts of the Intermountain West on Wednesday, when snow began falling three days before the summer solstice. Winter weather advisories were in effect for parts of northern Idaho and southwestern Montana early Wednesday, where snow in the higher elevations was accumulating as much as half a foot thick. Snow could visit Yellowstone National Park overnight into early Thursday, with highs near 60 and a chance of thunderstorms in the afternoon.

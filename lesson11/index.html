Climate Change
The possible consequences are daunting. The United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change warns that if the Earth warms an average of 2 degrees Celsius, practically all the world's coral reefs will die; the removal of the ice sheets in Greenland and Antarctica could trigger a massive rise in sea level; and in summer the Arctic sea ice, an additional shield against warming, would begin to disappear. At any geographic location, 2 degrees Celsius may not represent a radical change, but must be understood as a potential threat to ecosystems, landscapes and cultures. A recent study found that when the entire world crosses 2 degrees Celsius, the northeast could have risen to 3 degrees Celsius, with even higher winter temperatures.

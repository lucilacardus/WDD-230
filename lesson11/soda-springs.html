Thunder Moon, the third eclipse of the year.

The American continent will be able to enjoy the “Full Moon of Thunder” live, which will coincide with a penumbral eclipse. The event will occur exactly one month after the Strawberry Moon eclipse, which had to be seen online in this part of the world. The astronomical phenomenon will take place between the night of July 4 and the morning of July 5; and it will be visible throughout America and, although more subtly, in western Europe and Africa. Importantly, this event occurs when our satellite passes through the Earth's partial shadow (twilight), so the darkening is not as noticeable. However, if it is clear and the weather is good, it can be observed without the need for telescopes or binoculars. This time the Earth's twilight is expected to cover around a third of the Moon at the peak of the eclipse.
